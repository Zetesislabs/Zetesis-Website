I build systems that make discovery measurable and dependable: research-grade measurement, causal world models, and verification kernels—unified by Zetema, my philosophy of discovery that centers ignorance, imagination, and inquiry.

Motivation

I want to change the world to match an image in my mind—a world where discovery is faster, more reliable, and less hostage to luck. Zetema is the attempt to make that ambition operational without turning it into narrative: treat ignorance as structured, and treat inquiry as something we can design.

Three Things to Know

(how I work)

1. Discovery as an Engineered Process

I treat inquiry as a controllable operator. I try to make unknowns explicit, typed, and testable. The goal is not to sound confident; the goal is to stay corrigible while still acting decisively.

2. Measurement Before Models

I build and validate instrumentation and calibration stacks because trustworthy traces are the substrate of trustworthy inference. Most "AI failures" are measurement and calibration failures disguised as model failures.

3. Verification for High-Stakes Decisions

I translate theory into deployable systems that can audit their own uncertainty and failure modes. The output surface must be tethered to evidence, not narrative fluency.

Zetema: Discovery as Inquiry over Structured Ignorance

Core Reversal

Knowledge is what remains after inquiry. Ignorance is not a residual error term; it has internal geometry. If you can represent that geometry, you can engineer discovery.

Core Commitments

•

Ignorance is first-class: unknowns are typed objects (articulated vs unarticulated; resolvable vs structurally blocked).

•

Inquiry is a meta-operator: it changes not only beliefs, but language, variables, and mechanisms that can be expressed.

•

Robustness over confidence: use invariance-style reasoning—what survives future coherent challenges—not only probabilities.

•

Economics of discovery: design minimum experiments and highest-leverage hypotheses under resource constraints.

Calculus of Discovery

[epistemology]

Internally, I represent a local theory-state as a compact object that separates commitments, mechanisms, representations, and traces. Ignorance is tracked explicitly rather than hidden behind confidence.

Primitive Operators

Imagine

Propose variables/mechanisms; migrate unarticulated regularities into explicit questions.

Experiment

Design minimum interventions that collapse uncertainty cheaply.

Describe

Retype traces into stable representations; keep language consistent with data.

Communicate

Import external knowledge as traces/representations without forcing it into axioms.

Measurement Classes

Class Definition
Plausibility Could this claim be consistent with current structure?
Verification Is there evidence/proof for this claim against the traces and representations?
Invariance Robustness under coherent future inquiry (threat sets, stress tests).
Efficiency Validated novelty per unit resource.
Efficiency metric: η = ΔKvalidated / (E × Iprior)

Low efficiency typically signals missing causality, missing abstraction, or missing intervention design.

Cognitive Architecture

[noology]

A second axis of the work is the structure of the agent itself: representation bias and the capacity to discover new structure. Two agents with the same traces can diverge because they factor the world differently.

Learning

Supplies bedrock content. Tends to constrain you to what is repeatedly experienced and safely generalized.

Imagination

Ability to posit a structure cheaply from thin traces and then act to make it real— through tools, coordination, experiments, and deliberate world-shaping.

A serious system must keep ignorance explicit while still allowing fast synthesis.

Deep Dives

(selected projects with technical detail)

Industrial Pilot: Robotic Welding Reliability (Ather)

RCA + predictive maintenance under drift

Observed failure mode: Intermittent anomalies → frequent downtime → high MTTR due to ad hoc troubleshooting → weak linkage between alarm/defect and action.

Approach: Build a local world model for the cell aligned to the 4Ms—Man (operator context), Machine (telemetry/configs), Method (process recipes), Material (batch provenance).

Output: Ranked suspects, fault-path narrative, and minimum next checks that collapse uncertainty fastest. Drift treated as first-class; verification gates keep explanations tethered to evidence.

Sensor Validation + Causal Calibration (Temple)

Speckle plethysmography feasibility

Three workstreams: (1) Stable SPG reference system with primary flow/perfusion metric; (2) Reproducible multi-layer phantom with controlled confounders; (3) Calibration mapping with QC checks.

Thesis: Calibration is where sensing startups die; making confounders and interventions first-class makes calibration transferable rather than artisanal.

Terrapulse: Measuring What Models Miss

Research-grade soil CO₂ flux at ~1% typical cost

Low-cost NDIR CO₂ cell on embedded compute, designed to capture sub-minute flux pulses with ppm-level precision. Calibration stack is explicitly multi-layer (step tests to field closure).

Key principle: Calibration is not a one-time correction; it is a maintained mapping under changing conditions.

Formal Verification & Reasoning Infrastructure

I do not treat formalism as decoration. I use it as infrastructure: to make reasoning systems converge, to make ignorance explicit, and to keep explanations tethered to what can be justified.

Tooling: Lean4 / type theory / knowledge graphs. I also explore a geometric lens on model behavior (including LLMs) where homogeneity, impurities, and phase transitions in generalization can be treated as objects.

Current Direction (2025–Present)

At Zetesis Labs (ARTPARK @ IISc), my focus is to build decision and discovery systems that are:

  • Grounded in measurement and trace provenance (no mystery data)
  • World-model based (mechanisms, not just statistical correlations)
  • Verification-aware (typed representations and formal checks where it matters)
  • Deployable in physical environments where failure has a cost

Zetema is not only a philosophy; it structures work so each project increases the reachable space of inquiry.
